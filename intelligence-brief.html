AI-RAN Standards Intelligence Brief

3GPP Rel-19 AI/ML for NR Air Interface — Implications for NVIDIA Aerial
Source: RAN1 Session Notes #116 – #120 (Feb 2024 – Feb 2025) Generated: March 2026

1. Executive Summary

  • 237 formal decisions (agreements, conclusions, working assumptions) were reached across 7 RAN1 sessions, with beam management (47) and positioning (60) leading in consensus volume.
  • CSI prediction transitions from study to normative work at RAN1 #120 (Feb 2025) — a critical inflection point. Prior to this, 6 sessions were study-only. Ericsson's multi-step prediction results (N4=4) are the strongest in the field.
  • CSI compression converges on Transformer backbone (Case 0, spatial-frequency domain input) agreed at #119. Three inter-vendor collaboration directions (A: parameter exchange, B: direct transfer, C: fully standardized model) remain open — Direction C would most constrain GPU implementations.
  • Ericsson controls the positioning specification as moderator for agenda 9.1.2 across all 7 meetings, shaping every measurement format and signaling procedure. Nokia participates broadly but drives no sub-agenda.
  • The RRC parameter surface (R1-2501143) defines the exact integration points for NVIDIA Aerial: CSI-ReportConfig extensions, Associated ID mechanism, OtherConfig for UAI, and LPP extensions for positioning.

4.4 The SEP Tension

A critical pattern: 3GPP standardizes the interfaces (measurements, reports, signaling) but leaves model architecture as implementation-specific for NW-sided and UE-sided models. This means standardized interfaces wrap proprietary AI — creating SEP opportunities on the interface layer while the AI itself remains trade-secret competitive advantage. The exception is CSI compression (two-sided models), where the Transformer backbone is being standardized, potentially constraining all implementations including GPU-accelerated ones.

6. Three Interview-Ready Insights for the DevGAM Role

Insight 1: "The standard is creating a GPU-shaped hole in the RAN"

Across 237 decisions over 7 RAN1 sessions, the consistent pattern is: 3GPP standardizes the measurement interfaces and signaling procedures but explicitly leaves model architecture as implementation-specific. NW-sided beam management and positioning models have no standardized architecture — the gNB decides what AI to run. This creates a natural insertion point for NVIDIA Aerial: GPU-accelerated inference behind a standards-compliant signaling interface. The CSI-ReportConfig extensions are the exact L1/L2 API surface that Aerial needs to support.

Insight 2: "Ericsson owns positioning; Nokia is the broad-but-quiet participant — and they need different things from NVIDIA"

Ericsson moderated positioning across all 7 meetings, wrote every summary document, and shaped every agreement. They also dominate CSI prediction evaluation results with the strongest N4=4 multi-step numbers. Nokia covers all topics at every meeting but drives no agenda and favors vehicular scenarios (unique 100% in-car UE distribution). For the DevGAM role: Ericsson needs Aerial to be compute-dense (their multi-step predictions and positioning control demand heavy inference). Nokia needs Aerial to be flexible (their broad-but-differentiated approach means supporting diverse deployment scenarios). Same GPU, different value propositions.

Insight 3: "CSI compression is the existential decision — Direction C would constrain every GPU implementation"

The three surviving directions for CSI compression inter-vendor collaboration (A: offline parameter exchange, B: direct transfer, C: fully standardized reference model) have radically different implications for Aerial. If Direction C wins, the Transformer backbone already agreed at #119 becomes a mandatory architecture — every GPU implementation must run exactly that model. If Direction A wins, vendors keep proprietary models but exchange parameters offline, preserving Aerial's flexibility to optimize. This down-selection, likely happening in the next 2-3 meetings, is the single highest-stakes standards decision for NVIDIA's AI-RAN platform strategy.
